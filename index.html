The Collaboration

TUM x
Never Too Small

This study is a joint initiative between the Chair of Integrated Product Design at the Technical University of Munich and the global media platform Never Too Small.

Together, we are bridging the gap between academic rigor and architectural inspiration. We aim to understand how small, flexible spaces can be designed to support mental well-being and productivity in the hybrid era.

Building on Previous Research

The Scientific "Tiny Homeoffice"

This study follows our Summer 2024 project, where we applied principles of neuroaesthetics and architectural psychology to design furniture for micro-living environments.

The Research

What Are We Studying?

We're researchers from the Technical University of Munich. We are investigating how remote and hybrid professionals manage their work, environment, and digital tools.

Your insights will directly influence the design of next-generation tools that promote better balance, focus, and collaboration for remote teams.

Workspace & Productivity

Exploring the connection between your physical setup and your ability to get things done.

The Personal Touch

How you make a space your own, from meaningful objects to the hidden contents of your desk.

Space in Action

A look at how your environment supports—or hinders—the specific activities you do every day.

Comfort & Adaptations

The sensory experience of your workspace and the "hacks" or upgrades you've made to make it truly work for you.

Your Commitment

What's Involved?

The study consists of two simple, remote-friendly parts. We value your time and insights.

01.

5-Day Mobile Diary

For 5 working days, you'll receive a few short prompts per day (via a mobile app) asking about your work moments. This is flexible and takes only 5-10 minutes each time.

When: December - January

02.

60-Minute Interview

A one-hour, casual online video call to discuss your diary entries and dive deeper into your experiences. We'll schedule this at your convenience.

When: After the diary study
